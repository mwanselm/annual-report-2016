Feranmi

“I’m a third-year family medicine resident at Memorial Hospital in South Bend, Indiana. I attended college at Stanford University, where I ran track and field and instead of continuing to pursue track and going to the Olympics,

I went to University of Michigan Medical School. I found my way back to South Bend, where I’m heavily involved in the community and I have a five-year-old son, Alexander, who’s my pride and joy.”

“I’m a third-year family medicine resident at Memorial Hospital in South Bend, Indiana. I attended college at Stanford University, where I ran track and field...

and instead of continuing to pursue track and going to the Olympics, I went to University of Michigan Medical School. I found my way...

back to South Bend, where I’m heavily involved in the community and I have a five-year-old son, Alexander, who’s my pride and joy.”

“I was in my third year of orthopedic surgery residency at Yale and that’s when the accident happened. I jumped into a pool and broke my neck, injuring my spinal cord, paralyzing myself from the chest down.”

“I was in my third year of orthopedic surgery residency at Yale and that’s when the accident happened. I jumped into a pool and broke my neck, injuring my spinal cord, paralyzing myself from the chest down.”

Laura Nicholls

Guardian Case Manager

“After two post-accident surgeries, it was important to have long-term disability coverage to support his lengthy rehabilitation process. After his short-term disability coverage expired, he began to show signs of improvement.

And it was absolutely critical to continue his care at that point to help his chances of recovery, and that’s where Guardian was able to help.”

“After two post-accident surgeries, it was important to have long-term disability coverage to support his lengthy rehabilitation process.

to help his chances of recovery, and that’s where Guardian was able to help.”

“During my rehab process, I wasn’t able to practice medicine. I didn’t know exactly what I was going to do and that’s when Guardian introduced me to the idea of vocational rehab.

Not only did I get back to the old work I did before, but I was also able to get a master’s degree, which then opened up new opportunities, as well.”

“During my rehab process, I wasn’t able to practice medicine. I didn’t know exactly what I was going to do and that’s when Guardian introduced me to the idea of vocational rehab.

Not only did I get back to the old work I did before, but I was also able to get a master’s degree, which then opened up new opportunities, as well.”

Laura Nicholls

Guardian Case Manager

“He wanted to keep up his education in some way, so he decided to pursue a master’s degree of his own accord. And then he eventually went back into medicine.

He is the kind of client that makes us better at what we do by proactively pushing us to get the most of everything and everyone.”
